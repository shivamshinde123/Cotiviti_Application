RIVERWOOD HEALTHCARE CENTER — MEDICAL STAFF POLICIES
MSP-1. ADMISSION OF PATIENTS
The following information must be provided to the admissions office for every patient admission: patient's name, address, and home telephone number; name of the admitting Medical Staff member; admission date; provisional diagnosis; proper service or section; admission priority; gender and age; and means of arrival. In emergency cases, a provisional diagnosis must be stated no longer than 24 hours after admission. All patients admitted to a critical care unit must be examined by the admitting practitioner within 4 hours of admission.
Sample Admission Record:

Patient Name: John M. Hartford
DOB: 03/14/1958
MRN: RWH-2024-00421
Address: 142 Pinewood Drive, Aitkin, MN 56431
Phone: (218) 555-0183
Admitting Physician: Dr. Sandra Kowalski
Provisional Diagnosis: Acute Myocardial Infarction
Admission Date: January 12, 2024
Admission Priority: Emergency
Means of Arrival: Ambulance

MSP-4. MEDICAL RECORDS
A Medical Staff member is delinquent if medical records are not completed 30 days after a patient's discharge. On day 30, the member automatically loses admitting, surgical, and outpatient privileges. On day 35, a notice of impending suspension is sent by certified mail. Full suspension occurs on day 40. Medical records must be retained for a minimum of 6 years.
Sample Delinquency Case:

Patient Name: Mary Ellen Tran
DOB: 07/22/1971
MRN: RWH-2024-00389
Address: 87 Lakeview Road, Aitkin, MN 56431
Phone: (218) 555-0247
Attending Physician: Dr. Raymond Osei
Discharge Date: January 8, 2024
Record Completion Deadline: February 7, 2024
Status: Delinquent as of February 8, 2024

MSP-5. INFORMED CONSENT
Informed consent, as evidenced by a signed form, is valid for 90 days from its date. The consent form must describe the nature, purpose, alternatives, benefits, and known risks of the procedure including anesthesia. It must be signed by both the patient (or their legal representative) and the practitioner performing the procedure.
Sample Consent Record:

Patient Name: John M. Hartford
DOB: 03/14/1958
MRN: RWH-2024-00421
Procedure: Cardiac Catheterization
Consent Signed: January 12, 2024
Consent Expiry: April 11, 2024
Attending Physician Signature: Dr. Sandra Kowalski

MSP-11. RESUSCITATIVE GUIDELINES
Only three levels of resuscitation orders may be written: (1) Full Code — maximum resuscitation effort without reservation; (2) Code With Specification — only selected, clearly defined measures based on patient or surrogate wishes; (3) No Code (DNR/DNI) — no resuscitation in the event of cardiopulmonary arrest, though the patient receives the same level of care in all other respects. Resuscitation orders are not cancelled by patient transfers or surgical procedures.
Sample Code Status Record:

Patient Name: Mary Ellen Tran
DOB: 07/22/1971
MRN: RWH-2024-00389
Code Status: Full Code
Ordered By: Dr. Raymond Osei
Date of Order: January 5, 2024

MSP-14. EMERGENCY ROOM COVERAGE
The emergency room must maintain the following minimum staffing at all times: a current on-call practitioner roster; a registered nurse immediately available within the hospital; a laboratory technician on call; a radiological technician on call; and a respiratory therapist on call. The on-call practitioner must respond to ER call within 30 minutes.
