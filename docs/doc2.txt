RIVERWOOD HEALTHCARE CENTER — MEDICAL STAFF POLICIES
MSP-1. ADMISSION OF PATIENTS
The following information must be provided to the admissions office for every patient admission: patient's name, address, and home telephone number; name of the admitting Medical Staff member; admission date; provisional diagnosis; proper service or section; admission priority; gender and age; and means of arrival. In emergency cases, a provisional diagnosis must be stated no longer than 48 hours after admission. All patients admitted to a critical care unit must be examined by the admitting practitioner within 6 hours of admission.
Sample Admission Record:

Patient Name: Robert J. Callahan
DOB: 11/05/1945
MRN: RWH-2024-00512
Address: 23 Birchwood Lane, Aitkin, MN 56431
Phone: (218) 555-0319
Admitting Physician: Dr. Patricia Nguyen
Provisional Diagnosis: Congestive Heart Failure
Admission Date: February 3, 2024
Admission Priority: Urgent
Means of Arrival: Private Vehicle

MSP-4. MEDICAL RECORDS
A Medical Staff member is delinquent if medical records are not completed 21 days after a patient's discharge. On day 21, the member automatically loses admitting, surgical, and outpatient privileges. On day 28, a notice of impending suspension is sent by certified mail. Full suspension occurs on day 35. Medical records must be retained for a minimum of 10 years.
Sample Delinquency Case:

Patient Name: Linda Okafor
DOB: 05/30/1963
MRN: RWH-2024-00478
Address: 310 Cedar Street, Aitkin, MN 56431
Phone: (218) 555-0142
Attending Physician: Dr. James Patel
Discharge Date: February 7, 2024
Record Completion Deadline: February 28, 2024
Status: Delinquent as of March 1, 2024

MSP-5. INFORMED CONSENT
Informed consent, as evidenced by a signed form, is valid for 60 days from its date. The consent form must describe the nature, purpose, alternatives, benefits, and known risks of the procedure including anesthesia. It must be signed by both the patient (or their legal representative) and the practitioner performing the procedure. A witness signature is also required on all consent forms.
Sample Consent Record:

Patient Name: Robert J. Callahan
DOB: 11/05/1945
MRN: RWH-2024-00512
Procedure: Echocardiogram with Sedation
Consent Signed: February 3, 2024
Consent Expiry: April 3, 2024
Attending Physician Signature: Dr. Patricia Nguyen
Witness Signature: Nurse David Ferreira

MSP-11. RESUSCITATIVE GUIDELINES
Only three levels of resuscitation orders may be written: (1) Full Code — maximum resuscitation effort without reservation; (2) Code With Specification — only selected, clearly defined measures based on patient or surrogate wishes; (3) No Code (DNR/DNI) — no resuscitation in the event of cardiopulmonary arrest, though the patient receives the same level of care in all other respects. Resuscitation orders must be reviewed and reconfirmed every 72 hours during the patient's hospital stay.
Sample Code Status Record:

Patient Name: Linda Okafor
DOB: 05/30/1963
MRN: RWH-2024-00478
Code Status: No Code (DNR/DNI)
Ordered By: Dr. James Patel
Date of Order: February 7, 2024
Next Review Due: February 10, 2024

MSP-14. EMERGENCY ROOM COVERAGE
The emergency room must maintain the following minimum staffing at all times: a current on-call practitioner roster; a registered nurse immediately available within the hospital; a laboratory technician on call; a radiological technician on call; a respiratory therapist on call; and a pharmacist on call at all times. The on-call practitioner must respond to ER call within 45 minutes.
